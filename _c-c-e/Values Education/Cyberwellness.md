<h2><strong>Cyberwellness</strong></h2>
<p>Cyber Wellness refers to the positive well-being of an Internet user.
It involves an understanding of the norms of appropriate, responsible behaviour
with regard to technology use as well as knowledge, skills, values and
attitudes on how to protect oneself and other Internet users in the cyber
world. Any reported cases of inappropriate online behaviour will be investigated,
as the school takes a serious view of such cases.</p>

<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Netiquette</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>- Remember the rules of courtesy.
<br>Show people the same respect that one would expect from others both real
life and online. Avoid hurting someone’s feelings with what is written.
<br>
<br>- Be responsible for what is posted.
<br>Do not share provocative photographs or intimate personal details online.
<br>
<br>- Avoid flaming.
<br>Flaming is the sending of repeated, aggressive messages through the Internet.
Do not scold, insult, or ridicule others. Stay calm when ‘flaming’ occurs
and ignore or give time for the other person to explain. It may be a misunderstanding.
<br>
<br>- Respect other people's privacy.
<br>Do not read other people’s files and emails or spread unfounded rumours.
<br>
<br>- Respect other people’s time.
<br>Avoid sending or forwarding information that is not important or relevant
to others.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Cyber bullying</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>- Be Courteous.
<br>Do not post threats or slurs or insulting comments against others on a
website.
<br>
<br>- Do not spread lies and rumors about others via text message or online.
<br>
<br>- Do not post embarrassing videos about others on a site such as YouTube
or Facebook.
<br>
<br>- Do not impersonate others in order to trick, tease, harass or spread
rumors about another person.
<br>
<br>- Inform an adult or talk to a trusted friend.
<br>This applies whether the pupil is a victim or witness of cyberbullying.
<br>
<br>- Don’t respond to rude or mean emails or text messages.
<br>Online bullies get satisfaction when they succeed in getting the victim
angry or upset.
<br>
<br>- Report the bullying to their teacher(s) directly or contact <a href="mailto:cyberwellness@touch.org.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">cyberwellness@touch.org.sg.</a>
<br>
<br>- Stay away from the phone, chat or email for a few days.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Tips Dangers of Cyber Contact</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>- Protect your privacy.
<br>Do not share personal details like your name, age, home address, email
address, phone number, photographs or passwords when using chatrooms or
visiting websites.
<br>
<br>- Set your profile on any social networking site to private setting. Do
not accept any stranger as your online friend. Only chat with people or
friends whom you know
<br>
<br>- Only visit chatrooms or social networking sites that are approved
<br>by your parents. Use monitored chatrooms on trustworthy websites.
<br>
<br>- Avoid meeting a cyber-pal face-to-face without first discussing with
your parents or guardian and obtaining permission.
<br>
<br>- Say ‘NO’ firmly if someone is trying to persuade you into meeting
<br>up or giving personal information about yourself.
<br>
<br>- Leave the chat room if the conversation makes you feel uncomfortable,
weird, afraid or confused.</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Internet and Gaming Addiction</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>- Keep a balance between playing computer games and involvement in other
activities.
<br>Draw up a time-table for the different activities of the day and ensure
that gaming does not take up most of your time.
<br>
<br>- Act responsibly and complete your school homework or any assigned chores
before playing computer games.
<br>Remember that school lessons and other school activities must have priority
over gaming.
<br>
<br>- Set limits on playing time.
<br>Ensure that your gaming sessions do not become more important than your
school work, meal times and sleeping hours.
<br>
<br>- Take regular breaks
<br>After an hour of playing, you should take a 10 minute rest to break away
from the fantasy world of the game. It is also good to rest your eyes every
30 minutes.
<br>
<br>- Play educational games instead.
<br>There are computer games that provide exciting and education opportunities
such as cross-word puzzle, word search, etc.
<br>
<br>- Take interest in other hobbies.
<br>Get yourself out from the computer or handheld game. Play a ball game
or exercise with your friends to keep yourself physically fit.
<br>
<br>- Spend time with your family members and friends.
<br>Talk (face-to-face) to them about what is happening in school. It is important
that you interact more with people in the real world than with the virtual
ones.</p>
</td>

<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>Avoiding Copyright infringement</p>
</td>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p>- Check the website’s terms and conditions
<br>Always check the terms and conditions of use before copying, downloading,
hyper linking any materials on the website. You should follow the instructions
or guidelines stated by the author of a work before using it.
<br>
<br>-Seek permission
<br>As all materials on the Internet, CD-Rom and computer software are copyrighted,
you should always ask for permission to use them by writing to the copyright
owners. Similarly, you must also obtain consent from the owner before forwarding
an e-mail embedded with copyright materials or uploading copyright materials
onto their personal blogs.
<br>
<br>- Limit the amount of work to be copied
<br>Under the conditions of ‘fair use’, students are not allowed to make copies
of more than 10% of the total number of bytes in an electronic edition.
<br>
<br>- Best way to avoid infringing copyrights is to create your own artwork
or take digital photographs to use in your work.
<br>
<br>- Need Help with Cyber-related issues. You can contact <a href="mailto:cyberwellness@touch.org.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">cyberwellness@touch.org.sg</a> or
visit <a href="http://www.planetcrush.org/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">http://www.planetcrush.org/</a> for
details.</p>
